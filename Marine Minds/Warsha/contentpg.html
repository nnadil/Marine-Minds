Marine Life

01.Life Below Water

Marine occupy more than seventy percent of the Earth's surface and are home to what we might call a fragile system. It is an underwater world where marine creatures and plants are brought to life.

02.Marine Animals

Marine animals include all forms of creatures in the ocean ranging from the almost invisible plankton to the behemoth blue whale. Fishing, hunting, destruction of natural habitats and climate change are all threats that these individual, sensitive, and feeling members of possibly one of the most important and fragile ecosystems fulfill.

03.Marine Plants

One can easily start quibbling on if the ocean flora belongs to one domain or another, but there are countless of these species ranging from seaweeds to trees.

04.Human Activities

Humans interface with ocean by submersioning through scuba diving, fishing and involvement in marine conservation.

05.Human impact

Over fishing, loss of habitat, new found companion species, pollution, acidification, and warming are among the impacts of human activities on marine life and their habitats.

06.Water pollution

Water pollution occurs when harmful substances, often chemicals or microorganisms, contaminate a body of water, degrading the water's quality and making it toxic to living organisms or the environment.

07.Prevention of water pollution

It is therefore clear that the best method of protection of marine from water pollution is to have prevention at source.
